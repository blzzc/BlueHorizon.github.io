Overfishing

Billions of people around the world rely on fish as a source of protein and fishing is the principal livelihood of millions. Maintaining the balance of exquisite life in our oceans is just as critical to life on land.

But many of the world's fisheries have been pushed beyond their biological limits. Some are even at the point of collapse. Overfishing is a major problem globally, due largely to poor fisheries management and Illegal, Unreported and Unregulated (IUU) fishing.

Impacts

A new WWF(worldwide wildlife fund) analysis indicates that 85% of global fish stocks are at risk from IUU fishing, representing 10-31% of the world's catch, valued at $10-23.5 billion annually. This poses a threat to the sustainability of targeted species and the marine food web, endangers legal fishing, impacting coastal community well-being and global seafood security. The fishing fleet exceeds sustainable ocean capacity by 2.5 times, exceeding natural fish reproduction and leading to overfishing. Consequently, 24% of species are overexploited or recovering, 52% of fisheries are fully exploited, and key commercial species are endangered.
